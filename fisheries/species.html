Species Information

Sardine
A small schooling fish consumed widely across coastal India. Plays a vital role in marine food chains.
Mackerel
Known locally as 'Bangda', it is oily and rich in omega-3. Common in west coast fisheries.
Pomfret
A popular table fish, prized for its soft white flesh. Found in both Arabian Sea and Bay of Bengal.
Seer Fish
Also called 'King Fish' or 'Surmai', this large predatory fish is highly valued for its flavor and texture.
Anchovy
Tiny fish often dried or fermented. Important for small-scale fisheries and fishmeal production.
Tuna
A fast-swimming pelagic species. Yellowfin and skipjack tunas are common in Indian seas.
Indian Oil Sardine
The most commercially significant sardine species in India. Key to Kerala’s fishing economy.
Catla
A major carp species from inland waters, but sometimes enters estuaries. Staple in Indian aquaculture.
Rohu
Another important freshwater fish that can tolerate brackish waters. Cultured widely in eastern India.
Hilsa
A highly prized migratory fish found in the Ganges delta and coastal areas. Known for its rich, oily taste.
Barramundi
Also called 'Bhetki' in Bengal, this estuarine fish is popular in both eastern and southern cuisines.
Silver Belly
Small bottom-dwelling fish commonly caught by artisanal fishers. Used fresh or dried.
Ribbonfish
Long, slender predatory fish with metallic sheen. Common in trawl catches along the Indian coast.
Crab
Includes varieties like mud crab and blue swimmer crab. Widely harvested for domestic and export markets.
Prawn
Coastal aquaculture staple. Several species like tiger prawn and whiteleg shrimp are farmed intensively.
Lobster
Valued seafood with high export demand. Found in rocky coastal waters, mainly along southern India.
Cuttlefish
Cephalopod known for its ink and tender flesh. Important in Indian seafood exports.
Squid
Highly valued in export markets. Caught mainly via trawl nets in deeper offshore waters.
Eel
Found in estuaries and muddy coasts. Some species are eaten, others used in traditional medicine.
Threadfin Bream
Small demersal fish often used in fish curry. Common in tropical Indo-Pacific waters.
Goatfish
Brightly colored fish with barbels on chin. Found in shallow coral or sandy habitats.
Grouper
Slow-growing reef fish. Important for both artisanal and commercial fisheries in southern India.
And many more...
India's coastal and inland waters host a rich diversity of aquatic species critical to food, economy, and ecology.
